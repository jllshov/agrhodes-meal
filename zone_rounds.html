🧹 Room & Restroom Cleaning Instructions
Standard procedure for daily and terminal cleaning
☝️ Clean from clean to dirty, working from top to bottom.
  1. 1. Assemble supplies. Gather the disinfectant and cleaning equipment needed to clean the room.
  2. 2. Wear appropriate PPE before beginning.
  3. 3. Clean and disinfect surfaces. Using a cloth rag or sponge immersed in germicidal solution, thoroughly clean the following (change rags and gloves as needed):
    • Bed and mattress (Terminal Clean)
    • High-touch room surfaces: bed rails/controls, light switch, door knobs, IV/G-tube supplement pole (grab area), call box/button, remotes, bedside/tray table, chair(s)
    • Over-sink light (Terminal Clean)
    • High-touch bathroom surfaces: room sink, room light switch, room inner doorknob, bathroom inner doorknob/plate, bathroom light switch, bathroom handrails by toilet, bathroom sink, toilet seat, toilet flush handle, toilet bedpan cleaner
    Common restrooms and shower areas are cleaned the same way.
  4. 4. Clean the floor. Sweep, remove litter, and dust, then mop the room floor using a flat mop immersed in germicidal detergent to pick up visible soil.
  5. 5. Privacy curtain. Inspect, clean, or replace the privacy curtain as needed, and always during terminal cleaning.
  6. 6. Change rags/gloves and perform hand hygiene.
  7. 7. Check and restock. Confirm trash cans are clean inside and outside before placing a new liner. Confirm PPE, hand sanitizer, soap, and toilet paper/paper towels are stocked.
  8. 8. Mop the restroom floor using a flat mop immersed in germicidal detergent to pick up visible soil.
  9. 9. Clean your equipment. Using germicidal solution, wipe down the mop handle, mop bucket, and any containers utilized.
  10. 10. Remove gloves and perform hand hygiene.
⚠ Special Precautions / Isolation Rooms
  1. A. Use the proper PPE indicated on the precaution sign prior to entering the room, and remove it prior to leaving the room.
  2. B. Verify that the disinfectant you will use will kill the organism the resident is on isolation for. Know the disinfectant's proper contact time.
  3. C. Do not dust mop or use a dry cloth to dust furniture. Use a mop or cloth immersed in disinfectant.
🏢 Use the same cleaning principles for other public areas — e.g. lobby, dining area, and office area.
